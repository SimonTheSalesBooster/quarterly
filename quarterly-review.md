# Quarterly Review: The Interview

A 20-question interview that reviews your quarter and designs the next one. Three acts. One question at a time. No lectures, no coaching — just sharp questions and honest answers.

Adapted from Tiago Forte's Annual Review (ARC method). Modified for quarterly cadence by Strategy Sprints.

## How It Works

You sit down. The interviewer asks 20 questions — one at a time. You answer honestly. At the end, you get a clean one-page summary of your quarter and your commitments for the next one.

The sequence matters: gratitude first (so you don't start from scarcity), then honesty about what's real right now, then vision for what's next.

Takes 30-60 minutes depending on how deep you go.

## The Interview — Three Acts

### Act 1 — Appreciate the Quarter

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | What are you grateful for this quarter? Rattle off as many as you can. | Sets the tone. Counters "I didn't do enough." |
| 2 | What were your top 3 wins? | Forces you to name concrete achievements. |
| 3 | What were your 3 biggest lessons? | Extracts wisdom before it fades. |
| 4 | What risks did you take? | Tracks courage over time. |
| 5 | What makes you happy as a dog? | Pure, uncomplicated joy. What lights you up without thinking? |
| 6 | What makes you fast as a cheetah? | Speed, momentum, unfair advantage. What gave you leverage? |
| 7 | What was your biggest surprise? | Surfaces what you didn't plan for — often the most important signal. |
| 8 | One word for this quarter. | Compression forces clarity. |

### Act 2 — Read the Present

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|-----------------|
| 9 | What's hurting right now? Business, energy, life — where's the friction? | Names the pain you've been avoiding. |
| 10 | What do you actually want right now? Not what you should want. | Desires before goals. Honesty before strategy. |
| 11 | What's the one bottleneck that would unlock everything else? | The binding constraint — the Strategy Sprints lens. |
| 12 | What are you tolerating that you shouldn't be? | Tolerations drain energy invisibly. |
| 13 | What decision have you been postponing? | The postponed decision is usually the most important one. |

### Act 3 — Create the Future

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|-----------------|
| 14 | What would make next quarter your best quarter ever? | Opens the aperture before narrowing. |
| 15 | What's the single biggest win you want by end of next quarter? | Forces one priority, not five. |
| 16 | What habit to start? What habit to kill? | Behavior change, not just goal setting. |
| 17 | What do you want to learn or get better at? | Growth direction. |
| 18 | What do you want to do more of because it brings you joy? | Protects against pure optimization. |
| 19 | Theme word or phrase for next quarter. | A compass, not a map. |
| 20 | What's the one thing you're committing to — starting tomorrow? | Closes the loop. No "someday." |

## The Summary

After the interview, the interviewer produces a one-page summary:

- The quarter in one word
- Top 3 wins
- Top 3 lessons
- Happy as a dog (pure joy)
- Fast as a cheetah (leverage/speed)
- The bottleneck
- The commitment
- Theme for next quarter
- Biggest desired win
- Start / Stop (habits)
- The postponed decision
- Full Q&A transcript for the record

## When to Use It

Run it at the end of each quarter:
- **Q1 Review:** Last week of March
- **Q2 Review:** Last week of June
- **Q3 Review:** Last week of September
- **Q4 Review:** Last week of December (doubles as your annual review — run it twice as deep)

## Design Principles

1. **Interview, not worksheet.** Someone asks you questions. You think out loud. That's how insight happens.
2. **One question at a time.** No overwhelm. No skipping ahead.
3. **Gratitude before critique.** Start with what worked. Always.
4. **Desires before goals.** Know what you want before you plan how to get it.
5. **One commitment, not ten.** The last question forces a single commitment starting tomorrow.
6. **Compression.** One word for the quarter. One theme for the next. Constraints create clarity.
